How Much Energy Does Your Television Use?

Overview

Televisions are among the most frequently used electrical appliances in Australian households. With many homes operating multiple televisions daily, their combined energy consumption can contribute significantly to household electricity usage.

Modern televisions are more energy-efficient than older models, but factors such as screen size, display technology, brightness settings, and usage duration still strongly influence total energy consumption.

Types of Televisions in Australian Homes

The most common television types currently found in Australia include:

LED / LCD Televisions

LED and LCD televisions are widely used due to their affordability and relatively low energy consumption. Smaller screen sizes typically use less electricity, making them a popular choice for bedrooms and secondary living areas.

OLED Televisions

OLED televisions offer superior picture quality but generally consume more power, especially at larger screen sizes and higher brightness levels. Energy usage can increase significantly during extended viewing periods.

Smart Televisions

Smart TVs include built-in internet connectivity and streaming applications. While convenient, background processes and standby modes may increase overall energy usage if not properly managed.

Typical Energy Consumption (Indicative)

While exact electricity use varies by model, the following figures represent approximate average consumption:

  • Small LED TV (40 - 43 inch): ~100 - 120 kWh per year
  • Large LED TV (55 - 65 inch): ~200 - 250 kWh per year
  • OLED TV (55 inch): ~250 - 300 kWh per year

Actual energy usage depends on viewing habits, brightness settings, and hours of operation.

Reducing Television Energy Use

Australian households can reduce television energy consumption by:

  • Selecting energy-efficient models with high energy ratings
  • Reducing screen brightness where possible
  • Turning off TVs completely instead of leaving them on standby
  • Limiting unnecessary background app activity on smart TVs
